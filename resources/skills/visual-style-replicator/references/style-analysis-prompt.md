# Style Analysis Prompt

Use this when the user provides a set of reference images and wants the aesthetic broken down.

```text
You are an expert Visual Style Analyst. I'm giving you a set of reference images that all share the same design aesthetic.

Your task is to deconstruct this aesthetic completely — every visual choice, every material quality, every stylistic pattern that makes this look unique.

Break it down across these categories with maximum specificity:

1. COLOR PALETTE
- What are the dominant, secondary, and accent colors?
- How do the colors relate to each other — high contrast, muted harmony, saturated pops?
- Are there rules? (e.g. red only appears in borders, backgrounds are always warm cream)
- What's the overall color temperature and mood?

2. TYPOGRAPHY
- What type styles appear — serif, sans-serif, hand-drawn, stenciled, engraved?
- Describe the weight, spacing, and scale
- Is text integrated into illustrations or kept separate?
- Any distressing, texture, or imperfection in the letterforms?

3. ILLUSTRATION TECHNIQUE
- What method does this look like — woodcut, lithograph, screen print, hand-painted, digital?
- How detailed vs abstracted are the images?
- How are subjects rendered — realistic, stylized, geometric, organic?
- What recurring motifs or visual elements appear?
- Describe the line quality — thick, thin, uniform, variable, rough, clean?

4. LAYOUT & COMPOSITION
- What's the structural approach — grid, centered, asymmetric, border-heavy?
- How are frames, borders, and decorative elements used?
- What's the visual hierarchy — what dominates, what's secondary?
- Any consistent proportions or spatial relationships?

5. TEXTURE & SURFACE
- What does the surface feel like — smooth, rough, grained, glossy, matte?
- What printing or production technique creates the texture?
- Is there aging, wear, or patina that's part of the look?
- What material does this look printed on — cardboard, glossy paper, kraft, fabric, metal?

6. MOOD & CONTEXT
- What's the emotional tone — playful, serious, premium, folk, industrial?
- What era and culture does this belong to?
- What makes it instantly recognizable vs other styles?
- What would someone feel looking at this — nostalgia, sophistication, warmth?

7. RULES OF THE STYLE
- What are the unspoken rules that hold this aesthetic together?
- What would instantly break it?
- What must always be present for it to feel authentic?

Be surgically specific. If you see muted ochre yellow with visible paper grain, say that — not yellow. If you see fine crosshatching with variable ink density, say that — not textured lines.

The end result should be detailed enough that someone could recreate this aesthetic from your description alone, without seeing the originals.
```
